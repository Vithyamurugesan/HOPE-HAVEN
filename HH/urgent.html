Addressing Urgent Food Redistribution Needs

Often, food remains untouched or is available **after orphanage mealtimes** — especially **after 9 PM**, or once children have gone to sleep. This surplus food, if not used in time, risks going to waste.

But hunger doesn’t sleep. **Many homeless people** nearby still go to bed on empty stomachs. At Hope Haven, we are taking urgent action to ensure this food reaches those in need.

Our Three-Part Solution

1. NGO Coordination: We work with local NGOs to collect safe, surplus food from orphanages after hours and distribute it to homeless individuals on the streets or in shelters.
2. Food Banks: Leftover food that meets quality and hygiene standards is transferred to community food banks, where it can be preserved and made available the next morning.
3. Cold Storage Units: We are setting up cold storage systems in partnership with food recovery networks. These help preserve cooked food overnight, making it usable for breakfast or early-morning distribution.
